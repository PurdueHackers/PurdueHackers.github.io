HACKERS
ARE NOT CRACKERS

To us, the word hacker does not mean breaking into computer security systems. Instead we use the term "cracker" to describe those activities. We are a group of hackers who like to have fun, not some crazy black hat mafia. Here's how some of the best hackers in the tech world have to say about what it means to be a hacker:

"To the popular press, "hacker" means someone who breaks into computers. Among programmers it means a good programmer."

Paul Graham

It is hard to write a simple definition of something as varied as hacking, but I think what these activities have in common is playfulness, cleverness, and exploration. Thus, hacking means exploring the limits of what is possible, in a spirit of playful cleverness. Activities that display playful cleverness have "hack value"

Richard Stallman

CHANGING THE CULTURE
AT PURDUE

We think Purdue needs to embrace the kind of "hacker culture" that's so commonly found in places like Silicon Valley, Seattle, and Austin. Our mission is to bring that culture to Purdue by hosting events where hackers can meet and learn from one another as well as build amazing things together.

Right now most of our hackers are focused on hacking together awesome mobile/web apps and niffty hardware hacks, but we're always growing our collective skillset. So if you're even a little bit interested in any of those things, stop by one of our events and introduce yourself!

If you want to meet kick ass people and build cool shit, come to some of our events. Whether you're a seasoned pro or just getting your feet wet with programming, Purdue Hackers is a great way to grow as a hacker.
